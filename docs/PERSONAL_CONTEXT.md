## Values

I value work and artifacts that have:

- clear meaning;
- a real purpose;
- usefulness;
- joy;
- a mechanism or system feel;
- meaningful data or state flow;
- visible behaviour;
- a complete experience;
- honest trade-offs.

I prefer choosing tools according to the intended result rather than loyalty to a language, framework, engine, or community.

I do not expect every task to be exciting. I dislike work dominated by low-information busywork that is disconnected from understanding or useful progress.

## Personal development

The following context may shape the emotional tone of the website but should not be published automatically:

- I had a difficult childhood.
- For much of my life, I struggled to love or accept myself.
- More recently, I have developed a healthier relationship with myself through forgiveness, presence, and non-attachment.
- I have worked on separating my value as a person from productivity, work, projects, and professional identity.
- I have learned that narrow identities can restrict exploration, while overly broad identities can make commitment difficult.
- I prefer thinking in terms of capabilities, choices, and temporary commitments rather than permanent identity labels.

This information may support sincerity and emotional depth, but the website should not become therapeutic autobiography unless I explicitly choose that direction.

## Public-use rule

Before using a personal fact publicly, ask:

1. Does it reveal something meaningful rather than merely unusual?
2. Does it support the page or story?
3. Would I be comfortable with employers and strangers reading it?

5. Have I explicitly approved using it?
