## Visual language

The website is one physical, animated notebook. The notebook is the only primary content surface. A quiet desk may surround it, but content must not spread into a separate workbench layout or alternate between notebook, folio, manuscript, game console, and loose project-sheet shells.

The working internal direction is a `living blueprint notebook`: warm notebook paper carries real artifacts, and faded construction marks explain product behavior, system flow, mechanics, state, and decisions. Blueprint is a method of annotation, not a blue page theme or decorative technical grid.

Regular prose and interface text use Bright Chalk. Larger distinctive elements use Cartoon Relief. Pixel typography may appear inside authentic game media or a small project-specific label, but it does not become a second interface voice.

Games remains on the same warm paper and uses pixel art as printed, taped, stamped, drawn, or animated evidence inside the notebook. The current dark CRT and phosphor treatment is rejected. Do not use a separate arcade, console, terminal, game-menu, neon, or generic retro-portfolio world.

The site is intentionally highly interactive and should itself serve as a portfolio piece. Its physical vocabulary is unwrapping, lifting, sliding, drawing, rearranging, riffling, and settling paper. Movement should often feel deliberately low-frame-rate and stepped rather than smoothly app-like.

Possible elements include:

- crossed-out or rewritten details;
- slightly misaligned compositions;
- attachments and tracing-paper layers inside the notebook;
- small personal illustrations;

- photographs or screenshots attached only where the physical treatment supports their meaning;

The site should feel intentionally imperfect, not unfinished.

Use actual personal marks where possible rather than relying only on generic hand-drawn assets.

## Interaction rules

Custom interactions should:

- create delight;
- reveal personality;
- reinforce the content;
- make the notebook itself credible as an interactive portfolio artifact;
- remain responsive;
- support keyboard and touch interaction.

They should not:

- hide essential information;
- make navigation unpredictable;
- delay access to content;
- depend only on hover;
- interfere with accessibility;
- use unrelated visual effects that do not belong to paper, graphite, ink, blueprint marks, or the attached artifact.

The signature opening should feel like paper being unwrapped to present Igor's world. Later interactions reuse the same grammar:

- scroll may shift selected attachments, lift tracing sheets, uncover evidence, or draw a real blueprint path;
- click may leave a short pencil, ink, stamp, or pressure response;
- selected loose photos, notes, screenshots, or doodle sheets may be draggable within safe bounds;
- route and Work-mode changes may riffle, lift, or settle pages in a few stepped frames;
- text links and semantic buttons should look like writing, using an underline, circle, margin arrow, or drawn stroke rather than a filled box, pill, or CTA card.

The resting layout must be complete before interaction. Dragging and exact scroll progress never gate essential information. Reduced motion and JavaScript-free use show the final open, readable notebook. Direct links and browser history must never depend on completing animation.

Do not invent dates, weekdays, timestamps, locations, measurements, coordinates, or project notes for notebook atmosphere. Use them only when verified and useful.

Complete the desktop, mobile, motion, reduced-motion, and JavaScript-free storyboards in `docs/DESIGN_REFINEMENT_BRIEF.md` and obtain Igor's approval before implementation.
